COURSE OUTLINE

    • 1. Introduction to Early Childhood Education (this course introduce the foundations of early childhood education, the diverse educational settings for young children, professionalism, and plannign intentional developmentally approrpiate experiences for each child. topics in this course includes : theoretical foundations, natoinal early learning standards, NC Founations for early learning and Development, state regulations, program types, career options, professionalism, ethical conduct, quality inclusive environments, and curriculum responsive to the needs of each child/family.)
      2. Child, Family and Commuity (this course covers the development of partnership between culturally, linguistically and ability diverse families, children, schools and communities through the use of evidence-based strategies. topics in this course includes: the developing skills and identifying benefits for establishing, supporting, and maintaining respect, collaborative relationships between diverse families, programs.schools and community agencies/resources reflective of the NAETC Code of Ethical Conduct.)
      3. Child Development I (this course includes the theories of child development, observation and assessment, milestones, and factors that influence development, from conception through approximately 36months. emphasis is based on knowledge, observation and assessment of developmental sequences in approaches to play/learning, emotional/social, health/physical, language/communication, and cognitive domains.)
      4. Child Development II (this course includes the theory of child development, observation and assessment, milestones, and factors that influence development, from pre-school through middle childhood. emphasis is placed on knowledge, observation and assessment of developmental sequences in approach to play/learning, emotion/social, health/physical, language/communication and cognitive domain.)
      5. Child Guidance (this course introdues evidence-based strategies to build nurturing relationships with each child by applying principles and practical techniques to facilitate developmentally appropriate guidance. topics in this course includes: designing responsive/supportive learning environments, cultural, linguistic and socio-eonomic influences on behavior, appropriate expectations, the importance of communication with children/families including using technology and the use of formative assessments in establishing intentional strategies for children with unique needs.)
      6. Creative Activites (this course introduces developmentally supportive creative learning environments with attention to divergent thinking, creative problem-solving, evidence-based teaching practices, and open-ended learning materials while applying NC Foundations for Early Learning and Development. Empasis is based on observation of process driven learning experiences in art, music, creative movement, dance, and dramatics for every young child age birth through eight, integrated through all domains and academic content.)
      7. Creative Activities Lab (this course provide a laboratory component to complement the creative activities theory.)
      7. Health, Safety and Nutrition (this course covers promoting and maintaining the health and well-being of all children. topics in this course includes: health and nutritional guidlines, common childhood illnesses, maintaining safe and healthy learning environments, health benefits of active play, recognition and reporting of abuse/negelet, and state regulations.)
      8. Social/Emotion/Behavioral Development (this course covers the emotional/social development of children and the causes, expressions, preventing and management of challenging behaviours in all children. topics in this course is based on caregiver/family/child relationships, positive emotional/social environments, developmental concerns, risk factors, and intervention strategies.)
      9.Introduction to Exceptional Child (this course covers children with exceptionalities as life-long learners with the context of the community, school and family. topics ib this course is based on inclusion, legal, social/political, environmental and cultural issues relating to the teaching of children with exceptionalities.)
      10. Early Childhood Introductory Practicum. (this course introduces the students to early childhood settings and applying skills in a three star(minimum) or NAEYC accredited or equivalent environment. topics are based on observing children and assisting in the implementation of developmentally appropriate activites/environments for all children, and modelling reflective/professional practices.)
      11. Foundations of Education (this course introduces the examination of educational systems and the teaching profession. topics in this course includes: historical and philosophical influences on educatib, various perspectives on educational issues, and experiences in birth through grade12 classrooms.)
      12. Children with Exceptionalities. (this course covers a typical pattern of children development, inclusive/diverse settings, evidence-based educational/family plans, defferenciated instruction, adaptive materials, and assistive technology. topics in this course is based on the characteristics of exceptionalities and delays, early intervention/special education, transitions, observation, developmental screening, formative assessment of children, and collaborating with families and community partners.)
      13. Infants, Toddlers and Twos. (this course covers the developmental high quality, individualized, responsive/engaging relationships and experiences for infants, toddlers, and twos. emphasis is based on typical and child development, positive early learning experienes, supporting and engaging diverse families, providing safe, warm and nutrition interactions, and the application of the NC Foundations for Early Learning and Development.)
      14. Teacher Licensure Preparation this course provide information and strategies necessary for transfer to a teacher licensure program at a senior institution. topics in this course includes: entry level teacher licensure exam preparation, performance based assessment systems, requirements for entry into teacher education programs, the process to become a licensed teacher, and a professionalism including expectations within the field of education.
      15. Early Childhood Administration I (this course introduces principles and practices essential for the preparation and supporting child care administrators. topics in this program includes: philosophy, policies and procesures, NC Child care Law and Rules, business planning, personal and fiscal management, and NAEYC Code of Ethics Conduct Supplement for Early Childhood Program Administration.)
      15. Early Childhood Administration II. (this course is based on advocacy/leadership, public relations/community outreach an program quality/evaluation for diverse early childhood programs. topics in this course includes: evaluation/accreditation, involvement in early childhood professional organizations, leadership/mentoring, family, volunteer and community involvement and early childhood advocacy.)
      16. Educational Technology (this course introduces the ethical use of technology to enhance teaching and learning in all educational settings. topics in this course in based on technology concepts, ethical issues, digital citizenship, instructional strategies, assistive technology, and the use of technology for professional development and communication.)
      17. Language and Literacy Experiences. (this course provide evidence-based strategies for enhancing language and literacy experiences that align with NC Foundations for Early Learning and Development. topics in this course includes: developmental sequences for children's emergent receptive and expressive language, print concepts, appropriate observations/assessment, literacy enriched environments, quality selection of diverse literature, interactive media, and inclusive practices.)
      18. Early Child Capstone Practiculum. (this course allows students to demonstrate aquired skills in a three star (minimum) or NAEYC accredited or equivalent, quality early childhood environment. emphasis is based on designing, implementation and evaluating developmentally appropriate activities and environments for all children, supporting/engaging families, and modelling reflective and professional practices based on national and state guidelines.)
