World Civilization I (this course introduces the world history from dawn of civilization to the early modern era. topics in this course includes: European civilization, Asian civilization, African civilization, American civilizations, Greco-Roman civilization, Christian culture, Islamic culture, Traditional culture, Byzantine culture.)
      2. World Civilization II (this course introducesnworld history from early modern era to the present. topics in this course includes: the cultures of Africa , cultures of Europe, cultures of India, cultures of China, cultures Japan, and cultures of America.)
      3. Western Civilization I (this course introduces western civilization from pre-history to the early modern era. topics in this course includes : ancient Greece, Rome, and Christian Institutions of the Middle Ages and the emergence of national monarchies in western Europe.)
      4. Western Civilization II (this course introduces western civilization from the early modern era to the present. topics in this course includes: the religious wars, the industrial revolution, World War I and II, and the Cold War.)
      5. American History I (this course is a survey of American History from pre-history through the civil War era. topics in this course includes: the migrations to the Americas, the colonial and revolutionary periods, the development of the Republic, and the Civil War.)
      6. American History II (this course survey the American history from the Civil War era to the present. topics in this course includes: industrilization in America, immigration of Americans, the Great Deppresion of America, the major American wars, the Cold War, and social conflict)
      7. Hispanic Civilizatin (this course survey the cultural history of Spain and its impact on the New World. topics in this course includes: Spanish and Latin American culture, literature, religion and the arts.)
