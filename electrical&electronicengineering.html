    • 1. Analog Electronics I (this course introduces the characteristics of semiconductor devices and circuits.)
      2. Analog Electronics II (this course is the continuation of "analog electronics I". topics in this course includes : amplification, filtering, oscillation, voltage regulation, and other analog circuits)
      3. Digital Electronics II (this course is the combination and sequential logic circuit. topics in this course include number system, boolean algebra, logic families, medium scale intergration, and large scale intergration circuits, analog to digital and digital to analog conversion, and other related topics.)
      4. Industrial Electronics (this course covers semiconductor devices used in industrial applications. topics in this course includes: the basic theory, application, and operating characteristics of semiconductor devices.)
      5. Introduction to Microprocessors (this course introduces microprocessor architecture and microcomputer systems including memory and input/output interfacing. topics in this course includes: low-level language Programming, bus architecture, l/0 systems, memory systems, interrupts, and other related topics.)
      6. Communication System (this course provide the fundamentals of electronics communication systems. topics in this course includes: the frequency spectrum, electrical noise, modulation techniques, characteristics of transmitters and receivers, and digital communication.)
      6. Troubleshooting (this course covers the techniques for analyzing and repairing failures in electronic equipments. topics in this course includes: safety, signal tracing, use of service manuals, and specific troubleshooting methods for analog, digital and other electronic-based circuits and systems)
