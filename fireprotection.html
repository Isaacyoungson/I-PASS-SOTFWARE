COURSE OUTLINE

    • 1. Introduction to Fire Protection (this course is to introduce students to the development, methods,systems and regulations that apply to the fire protection field. topics in this course includes: history, evolution, statistics, suppression, organizations, careers, curriculum and related topics.)
      2. Fire Protection and Public Education (this course introduce fire prevention concept related to community and industrial operations. topics in this course includes: development and maintenance of fire prevention programs, educational programs, and inspection programs.)
      3. Fire Detection and Investigation (this course covers the procedures for determining the origin and cause of accidental and incendiary fires. topics in this course includes: collection and preservation of evidence, detection and determination of accelerants, courtroom procedures and testimony, and documentation of the fire scene.)
      4. Building Construction (this course provide the principles and practices related to various types of building construction, including residential and commercial as impacted by fire conditions. topics in this course includes: type of construction and related elements, fire resistive aspects of construction materials, building codes, collapse and other related topics.)
      5. Inspection and Codes (this course provides the fundamental of fire and building codes and procedures to conduct an inspection. topics in this course includes: review of fire and building codes, writing inspection reports, identifying hazards, plan reviews, site sketches, and other related topics.)
      6. Sprinklers and Automatic Alarms (this course covers the various types of automatic sprinklers, standpipes, and fire systems. topics in this course: wet or dry systems, testing and maintenance, water supply requirements, fire detection and alarm systems, and other related topics.)
      7. Fire Protection Law (this course provides the fire protection law. topics in this course includes legal terms, contracts, liability, review of case histories, and other related topics.)
      8. Fire Protection / Electrical ( this course covers the methods and means of electrical installations and uses. topics in this course includes basic electrical theories, wiring methods, electrical components and circuitry, and introduction to national electrical codes.)
      9. OSHA Standards
      10. Introduction to Wild-land Fire. (this course emphasis on the operation of tools, equipment, aircraft, and basic fire suppression methods.)
      11. Fire Fighting Strategies (this course covers the preparation for command of initial incident operations involving emergencies. topics in this course includes: incident management, fire-ground tactics and strategies, incident safety, and command control of emergency operations.)
      12. Instructional Methodology (this course provides the knowledge, skills and abilities needed to train others in fire service operations. topics in this course includes: planning, presenting and evaluating lesson plans, learning styles, usu of media, communication, and other related topics.)
      13. Chemistry of Hazardous Materials. (this course covers the evaluation of hazardous materials. topics in this course includes: use of the periodic table, hydrocarbon derivatives, placards and labels, parameters of combustion, and spill and leak mitigation.)
      14. Hydraulics and Water Distribution (this course covers the flow of fluids through fire hoses, nozzles, appliances. pumps, standpipes, water mains, and other devices. emphasis is placed on supply and delivery systems, fire flow testing, hydraulic calculations, and other related topics.)
      15. Emergency Management (this course covers the phases of emergency management mitigation, preparedness, response, and recovery. topics in this course includes: organizing of emergency management, co-ordinating for community resources, public sector liability, and the roles of government agencies at all levels.)
      16. Fire Service Supervision (this course provides the supervisory skills and practices in the fire protection field. topics in this course includes: the supervisor's job, supervision skills, the changing work environment, managing change, organizing for results, discipline and grievances, and safety.)
      17. Fire Service Personnel Administration (this course covers the basics of setting up and administrating the personnel functions of fire protection organization. emphasis is based on human resource planning, classification and job analysis equal opportunity employment, affirmative action, recruitment, retention development, performance evaluation, and assessment centers.)
      18. Municipal Public Relations (this course is a general survey of municipal public relations and their effects on the governmental process. topics in this course includes: the principles of public relations, press releases, press conferences, public information officers, image surveys, and the effects of perceived service on fire protection delivery.)
      19. Fire Protection Planning (this course covers the need for a comprehensive approach to fire protection. topics in this course includes: the planning processes, using an advisory committee, establishing goals, and objectives, and techniques used to approve and implement a plan. )
      20. Flame Propagation and Material Rating (this course covers the role of interior finishes in fires, smoke obscuration and density, flame speed, pyrolysis and other related topics.)
      21. Wild-land Fire Management (this cover introduces wild-land fire organization and management . emphasis is based on the incident command system and national inter agency management system.)
      22. Wild-fire Strategy (this course covers wild-fire strategy and the determination of appropriate wild-land fire tactics. emphasis is based on the extinguishing agents, use of ground forces and aircraft.)
      23. Managing Fire Service (this course is based on overview of fire department operative service. topics in this course include: finance, staffing, equipment, code enforcement, management information, specialized services, legal issues planning, and other related topics)
