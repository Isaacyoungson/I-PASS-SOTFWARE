COURSE OUTLINE

    • 1. Basic Maths Skills (topics in this course includes: properties, rounding, estimating, comparing, converting and computing whole numbers, fractions, decimals)
      2. Maths Measurement and Literacy (topics in this course includes: unit convertions and estimations within a variety of measurement systems, ratio and proportion, basic geometric concepts, financial literacy and statistics including measures of cenral tendency, dispersion, charting of data.)
      3.Algebra / Trignometry I (topics in this course includes: properties of plane geometry, are and volume and basic proportion applications, simplicification, evaluation and solving of algebric equations and inequalities and radical functions, complex numbers, right triangle trignometry, system of equations.)
      4.Algebra / Trignometry II (topics in this course includes: exponential and logarithmic funtions, transformations of functions, law of sines, law of cosines, vectors, statistics.)
      5. Quantitative Methods / Literacy (this course is to engage in complex and realistic situations involving the mathematical phenomena of quantity, change and relationship.)
      5. Statistics Methods (topics in this course includes: descriptive statistics, correlation and regression, basic probability, discrete and continious probability distributions, confidence intervals and hypothesis testing.)
      6.Precalculus Algebra (this course is to develop topics which are fundamental to the study of calculus. emphasis is placed on solving systems of equations and inequalities and analysis of functions (absolute value, radical, polynomial, rational, exponential and logarithm))
      7.Precalculus Trignometry (this course is to develop the understanding the fundamentals of the study of calculus. emphasis is placed on the analysis of trignometric functions in multiple representations (right and oblique triangles, vectors, polar coordinates, conic sections, parametric equations.))
      8. Brief Calculus (topics in this course includes : graphing, differenciation and integration with emphasis on applications drawn from business, economics and biological and behavioral sciences)
      9. Calculus I (topics in this course includes: limits, continuity, derivatives and intergal of algebric and transcendental functions of one varialbe.)
      10. Calculus II (topics in this course is emphasised on the application of definite integrals, techniques of intergration, indeterminate forms, improper integrals, infinite series, conic sections, parametric equations, polar coordinates, differencial equations,)
      11. Calculus III (topics in this course is based on multivariate functions, partial derivatives, multiple integration, solid analytical geometry, vector valued functions, line and surface integrals.)
      12.
