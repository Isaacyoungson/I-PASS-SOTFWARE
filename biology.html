COURSE OUTLINE

    • 1. Basics of Cell Biology (this course covers cell biology. topics in this course includes: biological chemistry, cell structure and function, cellular metabolism, genetics, and other related topics.)
      2. General Biology I (this course introduces the principles and concepts of biology. topics in this course includes cell structure and function, metabolissm and energy transformation, genetics, evolution, classification, and other related topics.)
      3. General Biology II (this course is the continuation of general biology I. topics in this course includes: organisms, biodiversity, plants and animal systems, ecology, and other related topics.)
      4. Introduction to Botany (this course provide introduction to the classification, relationships, structure and function of plants. topics in this course includes: reproduction and development of seed and non-seed plants, levels of organization, forms and function of systems, and survey of major taxa.)
      5. Introduction to Zoology (this course is the introduction to the classification, relationships, structure and function of major animal phyla. topics in this course includes: levels of organization, reproduction and developtment, comparative systems, and survey of selected phyla.)
      6. Environmental Biology (this course introduces the environmental processes and the influence of human activities upon them. topics in this course includes: ecological concepts, population growth, natural resources. and also focus on current environmental problems from scientific, social, political and economic perspectives.)
      7. Environmental Biology Lab (this course emphasis on the practical aspect of the theory of environmental biology.)
      8. Nutrition (this course covers the biochemistry of foods and nutrients with consideration of the physiological effects of specialized diets for specific biological needs.)
      9. Basic Anatomy and Physiology(this course provide the study of the structure and functio of the human body. topics in this course includes: the basic study of the body systems as well as an introduction to homeostasis, cells, tissues, nutrition, acid-base balance, and electrolytes.)
      10. Anatomy and Physiology I (this course provide comprehensive study of the anatomy and physiology of the human body. topics in this course includes: body organization, homeostasis, cytology, histology, and the integumentary, skeletal, muscular, nervous systems and special sences.)
      11. Anatomy and Physiology II (this course is the continuation of anatomy and physiology I. topics in this course includes: the endocrine, cardiovascular, lymphatic system, respiratory system, digestive system, urinary system and reproductive system. as well as metabolism, nutrition, acid0base balance, and fluid and electrolyte balance)
      12. General Microbiology (this course covers the principles of microbiology with emphasis on micro-organisms and human disease. topics i this corse includes: an overview of the microbiology and aspects of medical microbiology, identification and control of pathogens, disease transmission, host resistance, and immunity.)
      13. Genetics (this course covers the principles of prokaryotic and eukaryotic cell genetics. topics in this course includes the molecular basics of heredity, chromosome structure, patterns of mendelian and non-mendelian inheritance, evolution, and biotechnological applications.)
      14. Pathophysiology (this course provide a study of human pathological processes and their effects on homeostatis. emphasis is on interrelationships among organ systems in deviations from homeostasis)
      15. Microbiology (this course covers the rinciples of microbiology and the impact these organisms have on man and the environment. topics in this course includes: various groups of micro-organisms, their structure, physiology, genetics, immunology, and some practical applications.)
      16. Biotechnology (this course provide the experience in selected laboratory procedures. topics include proper laboratory techniques in biology and chemistry)
