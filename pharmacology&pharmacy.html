COURSE OUTLINE

    • 1. Introduction to Pharmacy (this course introduces pharmacy practice and technician's role in a variety of pharmacy settings. topics in this course includes: medical technologies and abbreviations, dreg delivery systems, law and ethics, prescription and medication orders, and the health care system. )
      2. Pharmacy Practice I (this course provides instructions in the technical proceedures for preparing and dispensing drugs in the hospital and retail settings under supervision of a registered pharmacist. topics in this course includes: drugs packaging and labeling, out-patient dispensing, hospital dispensing proceedures, controlled substance proceedures, inventory control, and non-sterile compounding.)
      3. Pharmacy Calculations (this course provides introduction to metric, aviordupois, and apothecary systems of measurement and the calculations used in pharmacy practice. topics in this course includes : ratio and proportion, dosage determinations, percentage preparations, reducing and enlarging formulas, dilution and concentrations, aliquots, specific gravity and density, and flow rates.)
      4. Sterile Products (this course introduce the intravenous admixture preparation and other sterile products, including total arental nutrition and chemotherapy. topics in this course includes: aseptic techniques, facilities, equipment, and supplies utilized in admixture preparation, incompatibility and stability, laminar flow hoods, immunizatins and irrigation solutions, and quality assurance.)
      5. Pharmacology I (this course introduce the study of properties, effects and therapeutic alue of the primary agents in the major drug categories. topics in this course includes: nutritional products, blood modifiers, hormones, diuretics, cardiovascular agents, repiratory drugs, and gastrointential agents.)
      6. Pharmacology II (this course provides continuation to "pharmcology1". topics in this course includes: autonomic and central nervous system agents, anti-inflammatory agents, and anti-effective drugs.)
      7. Hospital Pharmacy (this course provide in-depth study of hospital pharmacy practice. topics in this course includes: hospital organizational structure, commitee functions, utilization of referene works, purchacing and inventory control, drug delivery systems, and intravenous admixture preparation.)
      8. Community Pharmacy (this course covers the operational proceedures relating to retail pharmacy. topics in this course is based on general knowledge of over the counter products, prescription processing, business/inventory management, and specialty patient services.)
      9. Pharmaceutical Dosage Forms. (this course covers the pharmaceutical dosage forms and considerations in their manufacture. topics in this course includes: bioavailability, routes of administration, tablets, capsules,solutions, syrups, suspensions, elixirs, aerosols, transdermals, topicals, ophthalmics, otics, and other dosage forms.)
      10. Pharmacy Prof Practice (this course provides a general overview of all aspects of pharmacy etchnician practice.)
