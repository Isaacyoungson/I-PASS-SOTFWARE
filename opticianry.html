COURSE OUTLINE

    • 1. Maths for Opticians (this course covers the arithmetic algebra, geometry, and trigonometry necessary to evaluate optical formulas. topics in this course includes: signed arithmetic, evaluation and solution of equations, use of the calculator, and basic trigonometric functions.)
      2. Opthalmic Lab Concepts (this course introduces the operations of the opthalmic laboratory. emphasis is based on surfacing and finishing formulas, materials, procedures and equipments used to fabricate glasses.)
      3. Anatomy and Physiology -Eye (this course covers the anatomical and physiological functions of the eye and its associated structures. emphasis is based on normal vision and common disorders of the visual system.)
      4. Optical Dispensing (this course introduces the historical and modern dispensing practices and the laws governing opticianry, topics includes: basic eye choices, measurements, dispensing, adjustments, and record keeping.)
      5. Optical Theory (this course provides the principles of optics and opthalmic lens design. topics in this course includes : basic theory and optical formulas.)
      6. Laboratory Proficiency (this course provides preparation for the opticians examination. emphasis is based on speed and accuracy in all items on the competence list.)
      7. Optical Business Management (this course covers the basics of optical business management and current eye care trends and practices. topics in this course includes: professional ethics, inventory, accounting, personnel, insurance, advertising, litigation, equipment, and future trends.)
      8. Advanced Optical Procedures (topics in this course includes: advanced optical assessments and calculations.)
      9. Technical Proficiency (this course cover the preparation for the opticians examination. topics in this course includes the written portions of this examination.)
      10. Basic Contact Lens Concepts (this course introduces the theory of contact lens fitting. emphasis is based on rigid and soft contact design and fitting concepts.)
      11. Contact Lens (this course introduces contact lens fitting. emphasis is based on clinical applications, patient instrumentation, and corneal physiology.)
