COURSE OUTLINE

    • 1. Fundamentals of Occupational Therapy (this course introduces occupational therapy theory, practices, philosophy, and principles. emphasis is based on providing a basic understanding of the profession as well as beginning to develop interaction and observation skills.)
      2. Occupational Therapy Media (this course provides training in recognizing the therapeutic value and use of a wide variety of human occupations including basic activities of daily living, instrumental activities of the living, rest and sleep, education, work, play, leisure, and social participation. topics in this course includes: the understanding of different teaching and learning methods and styles, the language of occupational therapy, occupational therapy interventions including preparatory methods and tasks, and restorative and compensatory techniques.)
      3. Assessment Skills. (topics in this course includes: physical and psychosocial factors affecting performance, and sensory, range of motion, strength, co-ordination cognitive, visual-perception, self-care, and work-related assessments.)
      4. Professional Skills (topics in this course includes: professional ethics, supervisory roles, responsibilities and collaborative professional relationships, credentialing, certification and licensure; documentation.)
      5. Peds Concepts & Interventions (this course provides knowledge and skills needed for working with children from birth through adolescence. topics in this course includes: review of normal growth and development, habituation of healthy habits/routines, the role of occupational therapy with caregivers/providers, understanding of common conditions and developmental delays, and the role of occupation in assessment, intervention planning and implementation with pediatric populations.)
      6. Physical Conditions (topics in this course includes: medical terminology, common conditions, body functions that change disease processes, applicable theories and principles, assessment and intervention priorities for commonly treated conditions.)
      7. Psychosocial conditions (topics in this course includes: mental health conditions, applicable theories and principles, symptoms of dysfunction, assessment and treatment of individuals, planning and facilitating therapeutic groups, client safety, therapeutic use of self, and psychosocial aspect of practice.)
      8. Adult Concepts & Interventions (this course provides knowledge and skills needed for working with adults through the lifespan. emphasis is based on identification and discussion of common changes associated with aging, disabilities and chronic diseases affecting this population, assessments and intervention, including developing healthy habits and routines, and impact on participation in occupation in various settings.)
